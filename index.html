    • Fiction Stories

      • When I read the stories of Sudha Murthy and Ruskin Bond, I decided to write stories as well, although I made only two stories. My thought is To not only read stories and make one

      1. The Rubix Magician- The Full story

        2. Rubix Magician is an epic story blending historical conflict, supernatural powers, and modern heroism.
        It follows Ravi, a gifted Rubik’s Cube prodigy known as the Rubik’s Magician, who discovers mysterious powers after saving a championship from terrorists. Meanwhile, Amora, the reincarnation of a fallen British warrior seeking revenge, wages war against India. With the help of his possessed arm, housing the spirit of his late friend Teju, Ravi embarks on a quest to retrieve three mystical artifacts and stop the dark forces threatening his country.
        The story explores themes of legacy, sacrifice, and the battle between good and evil across generations.
        You can read the entire story of Rubix Magician Here

      2. Obsession- A Glory or A Misery

        3. Obsession is not just a story—it’s a descent. A fan writes letters to his idol, seeking recognition, comfort, and connection. But with each word, his mind fractures, his admiration curdles into fixation. When the final letter arrives, soaked in desperation and silence, a chilling truth emerges. The idol, Ramant, must face a twisted past, a betrayal that runs deeper than fame—and a revenge that refuses to stay buried. Behind the spotlight lies something darker.
        You can read the story Here

        Here is a comparison between Obsession- A Glory or A Misery and Gillian Flynn's Gone Girl
      Aspect Obsession: A Glory or A Misery Gone Girl
      Genre Psychological Thriller, Tragedy, Sports Drama Psychological Thriller, Mystery, Domestic Noir
      Main Conflict Fan obsession turns fatal; hero is wrongly blamed Wife disappears, husband is blamed; deeper manipulation unfolds
      Plot Twists Friends secretly murder the fan; memory loss and betrayal revealed Amy fakes her disappearance; manipulates media and everyone around her
      Main Characters Ramant, Rafhal, Aniska, Teju, Varshal Nick Dunne, Amy Dunne
      Protagonist's Journey From guilt and confusion to truth and justice From confusion and victimhood to fear and control
      Antagonist(s) Jealous friends posing as loyal companions Amy – calculating and psychologically dominant
      Emotional Tone Tragic, emotional, redemptive Cynical, twisted, intense
      Psychological Depth Explores guilt, obsession, loyalty, and justice Explores narcissism, manipulation, media impact
      Social Commentary Fame, mental health, blind admiration Marriage, media bias, gender roles
      Ending Justice is served; bittersweet tribute to Rafhal Dark compromise; toxic marriage continues
      Message Truth may take time, but it rises — glory is earned Truth is not always valued; perception controls reality

    • Few Songs

      • I took inspiration from Eminem in making songs. The first time I heard the song Rap God, I want to rap fast as well with depth meaning so

      Here are a few songs made:

      1. Acceleration

        2. Acceleration is a high-octane rap that captures the relentless drive to succeed in a world full of obstacles. With references to speed, gaming, and real-world systems like government and education, it’s both a personal anthem and a critique of societal structures holding people back. It is a sequel to one my songs titled Rap King


        Here is a song comparison between Acceleration and Eminem's Rap God
        Aspect Acceleration Rap God
        Main Theme Speed, ambition, self-growth, rebellion, tech/culture Superiority, lyrical dominance, hip-hop evolution
        Sub-Themes Critique of society, government, education, gaming References to rap history, internet culture
        Tone Bold, determined, rebellious Arrogant, aggressive, confident
        Flow Energetic, aggressive, with occasional switches Highly dynamic, multi-syllabic, fast and fluid
        Speed Fast-paced with bursts of intensity Guinness-record-speed fast in mid-verse
        Switch-ups Moderate—used for emphasis or transition Frequent—displays control over rhythm and speed
        Wordplay Creative metaphors, modern references (gaming, AI) Advanced rhyme schemes, puns, internal rhymes
        Vocabulary Modern, relatable, sometimes cryptic Dense, intellectual, tongue-twisting
        Punchlines Hits with attitude and clarity Overloaded with lines that double as flexes
        Emotion Urgency, rebellion, fire Ego, humor, ferocity
        Vocal Delivery Rapid and powerful, but more emotional Controlled chaos, flows like a machine
        Engagement Invokes connection with the listener Demands awe from the listener
        Audience Gen-Z, gamers, students, rebels Mainstream rap fans, hip-hop heads
        Message “Don’t follow rules—create your own path” “I'm untouchable—rap is mine to dominate”
        Legacy Original and evolving (my creative voice) Historical—one of Eminem’s most iconic achievements

      2. Odd Man Out

        3. Odd Man Out dives deep into themes of discrimination, isolation, and the pain of being different in a divided world. Through vivid references to global conflict and identity struggles, the song becomes a voice for those left on the margins.

      3. One Shot Of The Glory or One Shot Of The Misery

        4. One Shot of the Glory or One Shot of the Misery explores the fragile line between success and downfall. Inspired by real emotion and dramatic storytelling, it presents a tragic tale of a fan’s obsession, later expanded into the story "Obsession."

      4. I am the one

        5. I am the one celebrates unity and support among close friends. Namedrops like Rithvik Shetty, Rithwik Reddy, Arnav, and Sai Teja give it a personal, heartfelt edge. It’s about overcoming struggles together, no matter the odds.

      5. When Things are Unnecessary

        6. When Things are Unnecessary unleashes sharp wordplay and powerful metaphors targeting rivals Harsha and Royal Rithvik. Through clever name flips, nature-based imagery, and lyrical dominance, the track dismantles their ego while affirming Rakshith’s lyrical strength and the unstoppable force of his allies.
